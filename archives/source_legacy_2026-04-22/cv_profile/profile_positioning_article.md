# Profile Positioning Article

## Positioning goal

The goal is not to sell a vague "AI profile". The goal is to present a clear market-facing profile:

> Developer who turns business needs into practical tools with Python, React, and AI-assisted workflow design.

## What the market should understand immediately

The profile must be readable in a few seconds on LinkedIn, Malt, FreeWork, or direct business outreach.

The market should understand:

- I automate repetitive tasks.
- I build internal tools.
- I structure data and workflows.
- I use AI as a productivity multiplier.
- I can deliver quickly and concretely.

## What to emphasize

- Python
- React
- automation
- internal tools
- data handling
- document generation
- business value

## What to avoid

- vague "AI expert" wording
- overly academic positioning
- generic developer slogans
- claims that are too broad to verify

## Recommended public message

Short version:

> I help companies save time with practical automation tools built with Python, React, and AI-assisted workflows.

Longer version:

> I design and build business tools that automate manual work, structure data, generate documents, and make daily processes easier to use.

## Platform adaptation

### LinkedIn

Use a credibility-first tone:

- clear specialization
- practical examples
- visible technical base
- direct business value

### Malt / freelance platforms

Use a result-first tone:

- automation
- fast delivery
- concrete outcomes
- no unnecessary jargon

### Direct business communication

Use a simple promise:

> I transform manual tasks into useful tools that save time and reduce errors.

## Why this profile is coherent

This positioning matches the Solena methodology:

- start from a real need
- structure the workflow
- validate the risky parts first
- implement a practical solution
- keep the result understandable for the client

## Conclusion

The profile becomes strong when it is simple, concrete, and focused on business value rather than on abstract technology labels.
